Reading Activity

A child-friendly guide to the Gaza crisis

At school, home or on social media, you may have heard people talking about Gaza and have wondered, what is happening?
Right now, families are living through a war. Homes, schools and entire neighbourhoods have been damaged or destroyed, and children have been forced to leave everything behind as they flee the violence, often multiple times. For children, this means not only losing the chance to learn and play, but also losing a safe place to sleep at night.

What is the Gaza Strip?

The Gaza Strip is a small area of land in the Middle East, between Israel, the Mediterranean Sea and Egypt. The Gaza Strip is part of the State of Palestine.

The Gaza Strip is a piece of land in the Middle East that sits between Israel and the Mediterranean Sea. It’s called a “strip” because it’s long and narrow – about 40-kilometres which is roughly the size of a large Australian suburb – and home to over 2.3 million people, about half of them children.

What does the ceasefire mean for children in Gaza?
The ceasefire that began on 10 October 2025 gives children and their families a chance to start recovering from two years of conflict and reunite with loved ones. More food, clean water, and essential supplies, such as lifesaving medicine, medical equipment, gas for cooking and tents for shelter, are being allowed in. Humanitarian organisations, like UNICEF, can also reach more children with the urgent support they need, such as lifesaving nutrition, healthcare, clean water, education and protection. 

But a ceasefire doesn’t mean everything is fixed. Many children in Gaza have seen or experienced things no one should ever have to. They’re returning to places where their homes once stood – now reduced to rubble. Every child needs lasting peace so they can go back to school, play, and live without fear. 

How many people, especially children, have been killed or hurt?
Sadly, tens of thousands of people have been killed or injured, and many of them are children. It’s really hard to give an exact number because the situation keeps changing every day, but it does show just how dangerous and unfair the war is on children. Every child has the right to be safe, and that’s why organisations like UNICEF are working hard to protect them and are calling for an end to the fighting so that no more children are hurt or killed.

From https://www.unicef.org.au/unicef-youth/making-sense-of-emergencies/youth-israel-palestine-crisis?

Activity. Is this information true?

Read these statements about the situation in Gaza. Are they true or false?

Question 1

1. The gaza strip is part of Israel.

Feedback

False

The gaza strip is part of the state of Palestine.

Question 2

2. Many children in Gaza have lost their homes because of the war.

Feedback

True

Question 3

3. Tens of thousands of children have been killed or injured.

Feedback

False

Tens of thousands of PEOPLE have been killed or injured. But it is hard to tell how many children exactly.

Question 4

4. There was a ceasefire on 10/10/2025 and, since then, children are completely safe.

Feedback

False

A ceasefire doesn't mean everything is fixed. There is still a lot to be done.

Question 5

5. UNICEF can support the children in Gaza thanks to the ceasefire.

Feedback

True

During the war, support from organizations like UNICEF was forbidden by Israel.

Question 6

6. The Gaza Strip is a narrow piece of land.

Feedback

True

It's a long and narrow piece of land, to be precise!
