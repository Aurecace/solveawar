Learning to debate

Activity – Learn to debate

Approx. needed time
00:30
Participants
Individual task

Watch this video of a debate between a dad (Chris) and her daughter (Mackenzie)...

In this video, there's a clear structure that can help you organize a future debate. Can you try to complete the table with the content from the video?

DAD DAUGHTER
Conditions of the debate

The participants speak in turns. They have to explain their arguments while doing a challenge.
Solution If I win, Dad will play more videogames with me.
Opening argument I want you to play more outside.
Second argument I do enough exercise on Roblox.
Last argument Your mom and I can go pass more time outside with you.
Judges' verdict

Bonus!

What's the meaning of the following sentences?

  • "Her passion is gymnastics, so we got her a beam." (min. 03:58)
  • "I'm down with that!" (min. 04:33)
  • "You're going to be blindfolded". (min. 05:45)

Her passion is gymnastics, so we got her a beam.

 La gimnasia es su pasión, así que le hemos comprado una barra (de equilibrios).
I'm down with that! ¡Me apunto!
You're going to be blindfolded. Vamos a vendarte los ojos.

Think about it...

What is your opinion on the arguments of the dad and the daughter? Are they good arguments? Why? Why not?

Feedback

Chris, obviously, has much better arguments because Mackenzie is just a kid. She sometimes answer with arguments that don't answer to the problem. For example, it's a problem that she doesn't want to go outside, because it's unhealthy. She doesn't give arguments to support that she doesn't need to go outside.

During a debate, it is important to focus on the arguments of the other person. If you can't answer to an argument, he or she is probably right.
