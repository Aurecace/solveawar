3. Understanding and mediating

Let's put all into practice

We have learned new vocabulary and tools to help solve conflicts. Are you ready to put your knowledge into practice?

We will now try to use our new tools and knowledge to share opinions with people. Your knowledge of English AND Spanish can help other people that only know one language!

Think about it...

Has any relative or friend ever asked you the meaning of something? And what about you? How many times have you asked others?

Languages allow us to learn and understand more things from more sites. There are a million books, websites and videos in English that speak about topics that you love!

Of course, you could use an AI translator sometimes, but they can make mistakes that you won't notice... because you don't know English.

If I tell you that a video says something, I might be deceiving you. How can you tell that I'm not telling the truth?

Feedback

If you believe someone blindly, you can be easily deceived. The only way to be able to fact-check, is to understand the language used in that video or text.

You don't need to be suspicious about everything, but it's always good to verify things using different sources.

Did you know...?

To "mediate" means to help someone understand better another person (or something written or said by another person). When you help your dad to read something he cannot read, you are mediating. But when you help a friend understand that he is wrong about something, you're mediating too!

The person that mediates is called a "mediator".
