Introduction

Welcome to How to end a war, a unit that will teach you to use English as a tool to solve conflicts with non-Spanish speakers. You will learn to help two people who stopped talking to each other, to correct someone who says something false, and to defend others (or yourself!) when they are innocent.

Tell off = Reñir

Think about it...

When you were a kid, did you ever have a fight with a friend? Has your father ever told you off unfairly? Sometimes, it's your fault. Sometimes, you’re innocent. Sometimes, both of you did something wrong, right?

Feedback

The same happens when governments and leaders fight. Conflicts happen every day between people, but also between organizations… and countries. Most of the time, the two parts have a reason to be angry. But that doesn't mean they are right! Remember that.

Some recent conflicts that you might have heard about...

  • Catalonian separatism
  • Sri Lanka's anti-government protests
  • Russian invasion of Ukraine
  • Venezuela's election
  • Israel-Palestine conflict
