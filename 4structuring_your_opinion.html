4. Structuring your opinion

What's next?

Up to now, we have seen vocabulary and tools that helps us speak about a conflict. We have also seen how to mediate and how to answer to someone that is wrong.

Why should I debate?

Did you know...?

An argument is a reason that helps to defend a possible solution to a conflict. A good opinion is always backed by strong arguments. An argument used to reject the solution is called a counterargument.

For example, in the following dialogue:

– Mom, I want a PS5 for Christmas. I think I deserve it because of my good grades this year.

– I'm sorry, son. I won't buy you a PS5. If you had another console, you wouldn't study enough.

Can you tell which is the argument and which is the counterargument?

The argument of the son is that he has good grades. It's the reason he gives to prove that he deserves a PS5.

The counterargument of the mom is that he wouldn't study enough.
